# Analysis of Submissions — 與規管網約車服務有關的4項附屬法例小組委員會 (sc103)

Snapshot: 2026-06-22 (uploads through 2026-06-22; invitation closed 2026-06-15 17:00, but LegCo kept posting batches received around the deadline).
Coverage: all public PDFs read — ~20 organisation submissions + bundled volumes containing **≈1,135 individual public submissions with content** across four paper series (CB(3)662 = 423, CB(3)673 = 218, CB(3)688 = 378, CB(3)710 = 116). One scanned org PDF (汽車交通運輸業總工會, sc103cb3-710-11-c.pdf) was recovered via PaddleOCR. Only members-only (只限委員參閱) entries could not be read.

This is a major expansion over the 2026-06-11 snapshot (which covered the 423 CB(3)662 individual submissions and 7 organisations). The three post-deadline batches (673/688/710) roughly tripled the individual count and added ~12 new organisations.

---

## 1. Overall stance toward the proposed subsidiary legislation

### Individual public submissions (n ≈ 1,135)

| Stance | ≈Count | ≈% |
|---|---|---|
| Support regulation AND the rules as proposed | ~70 | ~6% |
| **Support regulating ride-hailing BUT object to specific provisions** (overwhelmingly the 10,000-vehicle quota) | **~895** | **~79%** |
| Oppose the regulatory regime / want it much tighter or halted | ~125 | ~11% |
| Unclear / no stance on the legislation | ~45 | ~4% |

The single dominant message is unchanged and reinforced across all four batches: **≈76% of all individual submissions (~860 of ~1,135) say the 10,000 vehicle-permit cap is far too few** — either raise it (most common asks: 20,000–30,000; full range 15,000–300,000) or abolish it and let the market decide (~210 submissions). This held steady batch by batch (662 ≈76%, 673 ≈71%, 688 ≈80%, 710 ≈74%), consistent with the Ming Pao report of "約六成挺1萬牌太少" (their count was more conservative).

**What changed after the deadline:** the post-deadline batches show a more organised *pro-taxi counter-mobilisation*. CB(3)688 in particular contains a bloc of near-identical templated letters (~35) demanding the cap be cut to ≤5,000 with a 2-year trial, and CB(3)710 has a higher share of pro-taxi individuals (~12–14%). The restrictionist camp is still a clear minority, but it grew from ~6% to ~11% of submissions and became visibly templated.

### Recurring specific points across the individual submissions (approximate aggregate counts; one submission can make several points)

| Point | ≈n | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| 10,000 quota too few / raise or abolish cap | ~860 | loosen |
| Taxi service poor (拒載/兜路/濫收/態度差/車殘舊) — cited as why ride-hailing must not be strangled | ~480 | loosen |
| Cap → longer waits, surge pricing, worse service for public/tourists | ~360 | loosen |
| HK lagging Mainland/Singapore/London; harms international image & tourism | ~235 | loosen |
| Quota is protectionism for taxi-licence holders/speculators (炒牌) | ~230 | loosen |
| Driver livelihoods: 20,000–30,000 active drivers exceed the quota | ~220 | loosen |
| Oppose lottery (抽籤) allocation — want merit/experience/activity-based priority | ~150 | redesign |
| Demand transparent, fast, metric-based dynamic quota review | ~140 | redesign |
| **Cap/disclose platform commission (抽佣，現約 27–30%); algorithm transparency** (NEW major theme) | ~85 | redesign |
| Insurance burden (want platform-level/per-trip cover instead of individual commercial policies) | ~90 | redesign |
| Oppose 人車綁定 / car-ownership prerequisite | ~65 | loosen |

| Pro-taxi minority (protect taxi livelihoods, hour caps, fare floors, cut/cap the quota) | ~120 | tighten |

### Submitter profile (individuals)
- Passengers / general public: ~75–80% (including a substantial, consistent cohort of expat/ethnic-minority residents — Western and South-Asian — writing in English; English submissions run ~16% of each batch and are almost uniformly anti-cap)
- Self-identified ride-hailing drivers / would-be drivers: ~10–12%
- Taxi-industry-aligned individuals: ~12–14% (grown post-deadline, partly templated)

## 2. The parties, grouped

*Tags: **loosen** (wants the regime less restrictive), **tighten** (more restrictive), **redesign** (change the mechanism).*

### A. Platform / pro-ride-hailing industry — POSITIVE on the framework, want loosening or faster review

**Uber 香港團隊** [CB(3)634/2026(01)] — *loosen / redesign*
- Welcomes the framework. 10,000 cap covers under ⅓ of its >30,000 active drivers; urges rapid dynamic review (≥quarterly) using P90 metrics with objective triggers; priority to experienced/active drivers (lottery only as tie-breaker); phased licensing; amend s.55T for platform "統括" insurance.

**智慧交通聯盟 (Smart Transport Alliance)** [CB(3)662/2026(01)] — *loosen*
- "Fully supports" the regime; calculates real need >15,200 active vehicles vs 10,000; wants reviews every 2–3 months (published); priority for existing drivers; ban resale/speculation; supports 人車綁定 with privacy safeguards; proposes a separate autonomous-vehicle pilot quota. (See §4 background in the published report: an establishment industry alliance, not a platform front.)

**滴滴出行 (DiDi)** [CB(3)688/2026(02)] — *positive framework; accepts the cap; redesign review* — NEW
- "Welcomes" the regime and, notably, **supports 10,000 as a prudent starting point** — a softer line than Uber's. Wants "小步快走" short-cycle review on supply/demand, wait-time and fulfilment data; accepts platform responsibility (盡職審查, 人車綁定, data submission). States it has operated in HK 10+ years and that its HK taxi-hailing orders rose ~330% year-on-year. Frames itself as complementary to taxis.

**高德打車 (Amap / AutoNavi aggregator)** [CB(3)710/2026(09)] — *positive; accepts the cap* — NEW
- The most pro-government platform voice. "Fully supports" the three-party licensing regime **including the 10,000 cap and 總量管控、動態評估**; positions itself as a neutral aggregator that lists both taxis and ride-hailing; offers navigation/dispatch tech and mainland/international tourist demand; recommends data servers be sited in Hong Kong. Endorses quarterly/2–3-month data review.

### B. Labour — POSITIVE overall (welcome legalisation), focused on driver protections

**香港運輸物流業工會聯會 (港運聯, 勞聯-affiliated)** [CB(3)641/2026(01); further submission CB(3)710/2026(01)] — *redesign / add protections*
- "Welcomes and supports" legalisation; accepts 10,000 as a prudent start but wants a review mechanism + monitoring committee and an anti-speculation crackdown; priority by platform record; protect peak-hour part-timers. Driver rights: commissions (27–30%) too high; statutory work-injury/retirement protection; a third "independent worker" legal status; platform-purchased third-party insurance; unified/transparent fees; rest stations and parking bays; tripartite consultation body; and (in the further submission) mandatory **face-recognition driver authentication** to stop account renting.

### C. Taxi industry — accept that regulation exists, but NEGATIVE on the package (want tightening / defend the cap)

**的士車行車主協會** [CB(3)634/2026(02)] — *tighten sharply*
- Taxi ridership halved (1.3M → 660k/day); 400–500 idle "死牌". 10,000 permits at once would be "毀滅性打擊". Wants phased issuance with downward dynamic adjustment; parity rules (age ≤3 yrs, fares not below taxi, markings); drastically heavier penalties on white-plate cars and platforms; data localisation.

**香港的士業議會 (Hong Kong Taxi Council)** [CB(3)641/2026(02)] — *tighten*
- 「的士為主、網約車為輔」. Wants 小步快走 quota releases; ≤20 online hours/week per driver; multi-stakeholder review committee (not waiting-time triggers); uniform insurance/inspection; taxi-style demerit + cameras; ban 禁區紙 commercial use.

**香港的士小巴商總會** [CB(3)641/2026(03) revised] — *tighten*
- "Four core defects": no statutory hour cap, permit fee too low, platform annual fee too low, no fare regulation. Wants a statutory 20-hr/week hard cap on a government central server; platform annual fee ≥$5M + 1–2% of revenue; statutory minimum fare ≥ taxi meter.

**的士司機從業員總會 (Taxi Drivers & Operators Association, 黃大海)** [CB(3)688/2026(01)] — *accept cap; tighten enforcement* — NEW
- **Supports** phased issuance of the 10,000 permits as a prudent staged quota. Wants hard platform/vehicle licensing conditions, continuous audits, heavy penalties on illegal operation and "外來平台" that "炒作配額不足", a ban on touting/taxi-rank queueing, anti-speculation, and incentives steering ride-hailing toward new-energy vehicles. Opposes one-off release of all 10,000.

**香港的士司機總會 (HK Taxi Drivers General Union)** [CB(3)710/2026(04)] — *tighten / defend the cap* — NEW
- The most systematic point-by-point rebuttal of the platform submissions. Argues the 10,000 cap is a legitimate supply-control tool, **not** a market quota to be raised whenever platforms report more active drivers; opposes dynamic review driven by platform data, the P90 metric, quarterly adjustment, priority for existing drivers, "先分配名額、後購車", and platform block insurance (says it hides the real risk-priced premium). Highlights disproportionate harm to **New Territories and Lantau taxis** (already range-restricted) from wider-roaming ride-hailing.

**智慧出行聯合商會 (Smart Mobility United Chamber of Commerce, 周國強)** [CB(3)710/2026(07); also the PDF filed as (08)] — *tighten* — NEW (but same taxi-owner circle)
- Uses electronic-meter telemetry from >1,930 taxis (week of 2026-05-11) to argue taxi capacity is structurally **under-used** — weekly occupancy ~29%, ~70% empty-cruising — so the ride-hailing quota "必須被嚴格壓低". Wants a taxi:ride-hailing ratio of no more than 8:2, a platform commission cap of 10%, a fare floor, HK-registered servers, and a ban on cross-border (北上) operation; pushes "的士網約化" instead. **Signed by 周國強, who is already profiled in §4 of the published report as a 的士小巴商總會 leader owning ~200 taxis** — i.e. the same car-owner circle filing under another banner. (Note: the PDF posted as paper (08) carries the feed title 順安車行有限公司 but its letterhead is again 智慧出行聯合商會 / 周國強, dated 2025-09-06.)

**新星的士同業聯會 / 新星的士管理公司** [CB(3)710/2026(13), (14)] — *tighten* — NEW
- Two short emails with near-identical 11–12-point checklists: phase the release (e.g. 3,000 then review every 2 months over 5 years), and make every condition match taxis (driver card, visible colour, passenger insurance, half-yearly inspection, demerit system, mandatory dashcam, fare ≥10% above taxi, no cross-border plate, no plate/vehicle transfer).

**汽車交通運輸業總工會 (Motor Transport Workers General Union)** [CB(3)710/2026(11)] — *tighten / anti-Uber* — NEW (recovered by OCR)
- A 106-year-old taxi-aligned labour union; "strong reservations" about the government document. Argues the supply/demand and "3 ride-hailing ≈ 1 taxi" capacity conversion is a gross misjudgment: once licensed, the 10,000 will operate near full-time ("特更"), equating to ~20,000 taxi-shifts and exceeding all 18,163 taxis, especially under platform subsidy wars (燒錢大戰). Stresses white-plate/Uber operation is **illegal** until the law commences (it cites a 2027-08-22 date), so giving permit priority to the ~30,000 current "active drivers" rewards illegality — wants **lottery (抽籤)**, not car-owner priority. Also demands platform commission caps (Uber 20–30% vs taxi ~10%), platform/owner legal liability (penalties currently hit only drivers/vehicles), a government-platform-labour tripartite mechanism, and that **Uber be banned/blacklisted outright**. Criticises the "先訂立後審議" procedure and an Uber-biased consultant who never consulted the union. (Text recovered from the scanned PDF via PaddleOCR; not hand-proofread.)

### D. Civil society / think-tank / political — mostly POSITIVE on legalisation, split on the quota

**香港文化教育聯合會** [CB(3)662/2026(02)] — *redesign (quality over quantity)*
- Affirms legalisation but argues hard quotas create artificial scarcity, surge pricing and rent-seeking; lottery is the least efficient allocator. Wants quantity control replaced by quality control (no cap; high entry standards; strong post-licensing supervision). (See §4 background: establishment culture-education body; suspected duplicate filing.)

**香港公共交通智庫 (HK Public Transport Think Tank)** [CB(3)710/2026(05)] — *loosen (abolish cap)* — NEW
- Strongly pro-ride-hailing and scathing about the taxi trade (the "業界" being car/permit owners who lobby for rent and fare rises rather than improving service). Argues point-to-point service must be demand-led; the 10,000 cap will strip income from ~20,000 active drivers and worsen passenger choice. Wants supply set by demand and employment, not a number; platform insurance kept flexible.

**公共運輸研究組 (Public Transport Research Team)** [CB(3)710/2026(12)] — *loosen / redesign* — NEW
- Attacks the quota's logic: the "工時" basis (3 ride-hailing cars ≈ 1 taxi) is meaningless for point-to-point service; with ~30,000 active vehicles and rest/maintenance needs, a 10,000 cap is incoherent and will recreate taxi-style 炒牌. Opposes 人車綁定 and platform licensing as anti-competitive (entrenches platform power over self-employed drivers); opposes the minimum-trip requirement as killing the flexible carpool supply that serves underserved NT districts (錦田/流浮山/新田/西貢). Suggests platforms manage driver vetting under a government driver database.

**新思維 (Third Side, political party)** [CB(3)710/2026(06)] — *positive + redesign* — NEW
- A long-time advocate of legalisation; supports all four pieces and the 2026-08-03 commencement. Accepts 10,000 as a **starting** number but wants a **statutory annual, data-based review** with results published to LegCo and the public, benchmarking taxis and ride-hailing on the same metrics; ≥5% of permits reserved for accessible vehicles (using reg. 15(1)(b)); a renewal minimum of ~30 trips/month; **tiered sex-offence screening** of drivers; and supports the existing platform fee level.

### E. Single-issue / technical stakeholders — NEW category

**香港殘疾人互助協會 (disability mutual-aid body)** [CB(3)710/2026(02)] — *redesign (mandate accessibility)* — NEW
- Single-issue: ride-hailing must comply with the **Disability Discrimination Ordinance (Cap. 487)** in full. Demands 100% accessible vehicles (auto/sliding doors, low floor, braille plates, talking meters), accessible APP options (WCAG 2.1 AA, ≤5-min wait, same fare), mandatory driver training, a complaints/compensation mechanism and a compliance subsidy — arguing the cost is <3% of platform revenue and far below the penalty exposure.

**香港保險業聯會 (HK Federation of Insurers, 保聯)** [CB(3)710/2026(10)] — *technical / neutral* — NEW
- The motor-insurance industry body (133 insurers, ~50 motor-authorised). Takes no position on policy form (to keep the market open) but sets out three models — extended private policy, standalone commercial policy, and platform-bought **blanket cover** — and warns that multi-platform blanket cover creates "Insurer Concerned Principle" claims-attribution complexity; insists ride-hailing premiums cannot equal taxi premiums but won't be cheap either, and that insurers need per-vehicle operating data (hours, trips, mileage) from platforms to price risk.

### F. Individual public (≈1,135) — see §1: overwhelmingly pro-ride-hailing passengers demanding a higher/no quota, with a growing but still-minority templated pro-taxi counter-bloc.

## 3. Positive / negative scorecard

Two axes: (a) attitude to regulating ride-hailing at all, and (b) attitude to the 4 pieces of subsidiary legislation as drafted.

| Party | Regulating at all | The rules as drafted | Direction of change sought |
|---|---|---|---|
| Uber | ✅ positive | ⚠️ negative on quota, lottery, ownership, s.55T insurance | Loosen / redesign |
| 智慧交通聯盟 | ✅ positive | ⚠️ negative on quota adequacy | Loosen (faster dynamic review) |
| 滴滴 DiDi | ✅ positive | 🟡 accepts 10,000 start; wants short-cycle review | Redesign (review cadence) |
| 高德打車 Amap | ✅ positive | ✅ broadly supports incl. the cap | Endorse + supply data |

| Individual public (n≈1,135) | ✅ ~90% positive | ❌ ~79% object to provisions (mainly quota) | Loosen (raise/abolish quota) |

**Net picture (unchanged, now better evidenced):** Almost nobody disputes that ride-hailing should be legalised and regulated. The fight is over calibration, in opposite directions. The taxi trade — now with more voices (的士司機從業員總會, 的士司機總會, 智慧出行聯合商會, 新星 two) but substantially the same car-owner circle (周國強 appears under two banners) — says the regime is far too loose. Platforms, the alliance, civil-society/think-tank groups, a political party and the overwhelming majority of ~1,135 public submitters say it is far too tight. New nuance among platforms: **DiDi and Amap accept the 10,000 starting figure** (only Uber fights the number itself), shifting the platform ask from "raise the cap" toward "commit to a fast, transparent, data-based review". The unions sit in the middle on driver welfare; insurance and disability bodies add technical/single-issue demands. The most contested single number remains the 10,000-permit cap; the second-most contested choice is lottery vs priority for active drivers.

Members-only entries are excluded. Counts are approximate (submissions often make several points) and the support-but-object / oppose-outright boundary involves judgement, but the directional picture — near-unanimous opposition to the 10,000 cap among individuals, mirrored by a smaller, more organised pro-taxi counter-bloc post-deadline — is unambiguous.
