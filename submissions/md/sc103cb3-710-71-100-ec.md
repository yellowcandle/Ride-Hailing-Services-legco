Subject:
有關網約車規管附屬法例的意見 —— 促請正視公眾出行需求與的士服務不足
Submission of Opinions on Ride-Hailing Regulatory Subsidiary Legislation — Urging Attention to Public Trav
el Needs and Deficiencies in Taxi Services
**正文 / Content:**
**立法會網約車小組委員會主席及各委員：**
**To the Chairman and Members of the LegCo Subcommittee on Ride-hailing Regulation:**
您好。我是一名香港市民，同時也是網約車（如 Uber）的長期用戶。得悉政府近日提出有關網約
車服務規管的附屬法例建議，我對當中提出的「10,000個配額上限」及「抽籤機制」感到極度擔
憂，特此致函表達市民的真實聲音。
Greetings. As a Hong Kong citizen and a long-term user of ride-hailing services (such as Uber), I am writing to
express my grave concerns regarding the government's recently proposed subsidiary legislation for ride-hailing
services, particularly the "10,000 quota cap" and the "lottery system."
### **一、 傳統的士服務的長期不足 / 1. Long-standing Deficiencies of Traditional Taxi Services**
市民之所以高度依賴網約車，核心原因在於傳統的士服務長期未能與時並進，存在諸多令市民無
奈的不足之處
The core reason why citizens rely heavily on ride-hailing services is that traditional taxis have long failed to ke
ep pace with the times, presenting numerous persistent deficiencies that frustrate the public:
* **拒載與揀客問題嚴重 (Severe Refusal of Hires and "Cherry-picking")**
傳統的士在交更時段、過海、或前往偏遠地區（如新界鄉郊、深宵偏遠屋苑）時，經常出現拒
載或揀客情況，令市民在最需要車輛時求助無門。
Traditional taxis frequently refuse hires or cherry-pick passengers during shift changes, cross-harbor trips, or
when asked to go to remote areas (such as rural New Territories or remote estates late at night), leaving citizens
stranded when they need transport the most.
收費不透明與濫收車資 (Opaque Pricing and Overcharging)
部分不良的士司機在惡劣天氣（如颱風、暴雨）或節假日期間肆意濫收車資（俗稱「黑
的」）。相反，網約車提供**預先報價**，收費完全公開透明，保障消費者權益。
Some rogue taxi drivers flagrantly overcharge (commonly known as "black taxis") during adverse weather (su
ch as typhoons or heavy rain) or public holidays. In contrast, ride-hailing platforms provide **upfront pricing*
*, ensuring complete transparency and protecting consumer rights.
* **電子支付落後 (Outdated Payment Options)**

在智慧城市發展下，絕大多數傳統的士仍強制要求現金支付，或以「無找續」為由拒絕大額鈔
票，極不方便市民及訪港旅客。網約車則全面支援無現金電子支付。
Despite Hong Kong's smart city ambitions, the vast majority of traditional taxis still mandate cash payments o
r refuse large bills claiming they have "no change," which is highly inconvenient for locals and tourists alike. R
ide-hailing platforms fully support seamless cashless transactions.
* **車廂環境與服務態度參差 (Substandard Vehicle Conditions and Poor Service Attitude)**
傳統的士車齡普遍偏高，車廂衛生環境不理想，且部分司機服務態度惡劣。而網約車擁有雙向
評分機制，能有效約束司機，提供更乾淨、舒適且有禮貌的乘車體驗。
The average age of traditional taxis is relatively high, vehicle hygiene is often unsatisfactory, and the service
attitude of some drivers remains heavily criticized. Ride-hailing services utilize a two-way rating system that ef
fectively incentivizes drivers to maintain a clean, comfortable, and polite environment.
### **二、 對新法例建議的強烈隱憂 / 2. Grave Concerns Over the Proposed Regulations**
基於上述的士服務的不足，新規管建議將對市民帶來進一步打擊：
Given the shortcomings of taxis mentioned above, the new regulatory proposals will deal a further blow to the
public:
* **人為限制供應，導致出行更難、更貴 (Artificially Restricted Supply Leading to Costlier and Harder
Travel)**
10,000 個配額顯然遠低於市場實際需求。限制供應將導致供求嚴重失衡，未來市民等候配對車
輛的時間勢必大幅延長，動態定價更會推高車費，將成本完全轉嫁給乘客。
A cap of 10,000 permits is blatantly insufficient to meet actual market demand. Restricting supply will trigger
a severe demand-supply mismatch, drastically prolonging waiting times and driving up fares via dynamic pricin
g, effectively transferring all regulatory costs to passengers.
* **「抽籤機制」流於運氣，漠視服務質素 (The "Lottery System" Relies on Luck and Ignores Service Q
uality)**
以抽籤形式分配配額是本末倒置的做法。發牌機制理應以司機的服務質素、安全紀錄為審批標
準，而非單憑運氣，否則完全無法保障市民獲得優質的交通服務。
Allocating quotas via a lottery system is counterproductive. The licensing mechanism should be heavily base
d on objective criteria such as a driver's service quality and safety records rather than pure luck; otherwise, it gu
arantees no improvement in transport service standards for citizens.
### **結論與強烈呼籲 / Conclusion and Urgent Appeal**
規管網約車的初衷理應是保障乘客利益、引入良性競爭以逼使傳統的士提升服務質素。若為了保
護單一行業的既得利益而強行扼殺網約車，最終受苦的只有香港市民。我強烈呼籲立法會放寬或
取消 10,000 個配額上限，並廢除抽籤機制，以市民出行福祉為依歸。
The original intent of regulating ride-hailing services should be to protect passenger interests and introduce hea
lthy competition to compel traditional taxis to upgrade their service quality. If ride-hailing is stifled simply to pr

otect the vested interests of a single industry, it is the citizens of Hong Kong who will Ultimately suffer. I stron
gly urge the Legislative Council to relax or remove the 10,000 quota cap and abolish the lottery system, placing
the commuting welfare of the public above all else.
謝謝。
Thank you.
一名重視出行選擇的香港市民 敬上
Yours faithfully,
A Hong Kong Citizen who Values Travel Choices

主旨: Statement in Support of Uncapped Ride-Hailing Services in Hong Kong

Dear Sirs
We are living in the 21st century. Hong Kong has long prided itself on being Asia's world city, a place
that champions progressive outlooks, open competition, and forward-thinking governance. That
progressiveness is precisely what gave Hong Kong its strength and success: free markets, consumer
choice, and the relentless pursuit of excellence over protectionism.
Yet when it comes to urban transport, Hong Kong is embarrassingly stuck in the past. The Hong Kong
Taxi Owners' Association Limited and its counterparts have mismanaged their civil duty to such an
extent that they no longer deserve exclusivity in this 21st century free market. Their services are
appalling. Elderly drivers who are clearly not fit to drive continue to operate, as evidenced by the
alarming number of crashes and accidents, some of them fatal. Bad and aggressive driving is routine.
Vehicles are old, poorly maintained, and not kept up to date. Service is rude, with drivers selectively
choosing whom to pick up, overcharging passengers, and declining to drive to destinations they are duty
bound to serve.
These associations are corrupt, hiding behind protectionism within the Hong Kong government, which is
shocking and shameful. It reflects extremely badly on the Hong Kong government establishment. This
obvious grift is at risk of undermining public trust in the government itself, because citizens can see
exactly what is happening: a privileged cartel being shielded from competition while the public suffers.
What is seemingly being overlooked in this entire debate is the end customer: the people of Hong Kong,
whom taxi and ride-sharing services exist to serve. What good does limiting vehicle numbers do except
to frustrate ordinary people and drive up prices through artificial scarcity and overcharging? It also
pushes more residents to buy private cars and hire drivers who then illegally double park and clog up
the roads, making traffic worse for everyone. This needs to be penalised, whilst the government should
be promoting multi-person vehicular use that actually reduces congestion.
Safety should be paramount. There must be a mandatory retirement age of 65 years old for all
commercial drivers. There must be regular inspections of all commercial vehicles, including the
installation of tachometers to ensure drivers have not been speeding, driving dangerously, or operating
without adequate rest and indication.
Anything less than this will cause a public outcry that the government is not acting in the best interests
of the people, and is implicit in some kind of corruption. The time for reform is now. Open the market to
ride-hailing services, hold the incumbent taxi industry to proper standards, and put the citizens of Hong
Kong first.
Sincerely,
Andrew Massey
Happy Valley

主旨: 關於將商用車輛（包括擬立法規管的網約車）駕駛執照考取最低年齡由 21 歲下調至 18 歲的建議

建議書

主題：關於將商用車輛（包括擬立法規管的網約車）駕駛執照考取最低年齡由 21 歲下調至 18 歲的
建議

一、背景

現時香港法例規定，申請商用車輛（包括擬立法規管的網約車）駕駛執照的最低年齡為 21 歲，並須
持有有效私家車或輕型貨車執照至少一年。此規定旨在確保申請人具備足夠駕駛經驗。然而，隨著
社會經濟及勞動市場變化，此年齡限制或未充分考慮年輕人的就業需求及行業人力短缺問題。

二、建議內容

建議在不改變「一年駕駛經驗」的前提下，將商用車牌照的考取最低年齡由 21 歲下調至 18 歲。
即：年滿 18 歲、持有私家車／輕型貨車執照滿一年者，可報考商用車輛執照。

三、理據

1. 配合法定成年責任及國際趨勢
香港 18 歲已屬法定成年人，可獨立簽署合約、投票及承擔刑事責任。鄰近地區如中國內地（20 歲
可考取巴士執照，部分商用車 18 歲）、英國（18 歲可考貨車／巴士執照）等，均允許 18 歲起逐步
考取商用車牌。下調年齡能與國際標準接軌。
2. 紓緩運輸業人手短缺

香港運輸業長期面對司機高齡化及人手不足問題，尤其輕型貨車、小巴等行業。放寬年齡可吸引更
多有志投身物流、運輸的年輕人提早入行，累積專業經驗，有助行業長遠發展。
3. 提前培育專業駕駛人才
18 歲年輕人學習及適應能力較強，提早考取商用車牌可配合職業訓練，例如在 19 歲時即可正式投
入工作，縮短行業培訓空窗期，提升整體駕駛安全及專業水平。
4. 「一年駕齡」條件維持，風險可控
建議保留一年私家車駕駛經驗要求，確保申請人已基本掌握道路安全意識及操控技巧。與現行 21
歲者相比，18 歲申請人同樣需通過嚴格體檢、筆試及路試，安全標準並無降低。
5. 促進年輕人就業及向上流動
部分年輕人未打算升讀大學，及早取得商用車牌可任職速遞、貨運、公共運輸等職位，提供多元就
業出路，減低青年失業風險，亦有助減輕家庭經濟負擔。

四、建議配套措施

· 要求駕駛學校增設 18 至 20 歲商用車專注課程，強化防衛駕駛及疲勞管理訓練。
· 修例後設 兩年試行期，期間須定期向立法會提交安全統計報告。

五、結論

下調商用車考牌年齡至 18 歲，並維持一年駕齡條件，既能回應行業需求及青年就業需要，亦不降低
道路安全門檻。懇請 貴局積極考慮此建議，並與業界及公眾諮詢商討。
