the younger generation by creating viable, flexible employment opportunities,
helping to rejuvenate the local transport pool.

compared to global and regional benchmarks. For instance, while Japan maintains

revolutionized urban mobility through a citizen-centric, technology-first approach.

the United States, Europe, and Australia. They have universally expressed shock

benefit for our tourism industry or our standing as an international financial hub.

and the modernization of our city. The government must actively seek out and
listen to the feedback of ordinary citizens and international residents alike.

香港規管網約車服務的意見：
本人作為經常使用網約車服務的市民，特此就政府規管網約車的政策提出以
下意見。
一、的士服務不足，市民需求迫切
香港的士服務長期存在拒載、態度不佳等問題，尤其在打風落雨時更是一車
難求。這種情況令市民在日常出行中缺乏可靠選擇。相比之下，網約車提供
更高質素的服務，能有效填補的士服務不足的缺口。
二、安全疑慮不容忽視
每年涉及的士的交通意外多不勝數，當中不少為嚴重事故，甚至導致死亡。
作為有經濟能力的市民，在香港這個高度發展的大都會，難道只能被迫選擇
的士嗎？網約車平台在司機管理、車輛監控及乘客保障方面更具透明度，能
提升道路安全。
三、發牌數量不足，影響市民選擇
政府若僅發出一萬個網約車牌照，根本不足以應付龐大需求。此舉只會迫使
一批有經濟能力但不願放棄安全的市民自行購車，增加道路壓力與環境負擔
。相信政府亦不願見到此情況發生。
四、國際形象與城市定位
全球網約車服務盛行，香港作為國際大都會，若僅限一萬個牌照，實屬國際
笑話，將嚴重影響本港的國際形象與競爭力。香港應與國際接軌，展現開放
、進步的城市定位。

五、彈性工作精神不可抹殺
網約車的精神在於司機可彈性選擇工作時間。若因發牌限制而強制司機每天
必須工作六小時，實屬弄巧反絕，將大大減少高質司機及車輛的參與意欲。
若要全職才獲發牌，效果與增加的士牌無異，完全背離共享運輸的理念。
六、自由選擇與經濟發展
網約車代表市民在自由社會中的選擇權。優質服務淘汰劣質服務是自然經濟
定律，社會才能進步。若有經濟能力的市民仍不能選擇更好服務，便是限制
自由經濟活動，阻礙GDP增長，百害而無一利。
七、建議
增加網約車發牌數量，以滿足市民日常需求，避免迫使市民自行購車。
取消或放寬每日工作時數限制，保留網約車彈性工作的精神。以市民安全及
選擇權為優先，而非單純滿足的士行業利益。
與國際接軌，確保香港在共享運輸領域不落後，維護本港國際形象。
結語網約車不僅是交通工具，更是香港市民自由選擇與城市進步的象徵。若
政府因顧及的士行業而犧牲市民的選擇權、安全及國際形象，實在得不償失
。故此，懇請政府再三考慮，增加網約車發牌數量，減少不必要的限制，讓
香港真正成為一個現代化、自由且安全的大都會。

This submission strongly urges the Legislative Council Subcommittee to increase
the initial ride-hailing vehicle permit quota from 10,000 to not less than 20,000 or
more. While the formal regulation of the sector is highly welcomed, the proposed
10,000 cap is overly restrictive and detached from current market realities.
The core justifications for raising the quota to at least 20,000 include:
Meeting Actual Consumer Demand: Ride-hailing has become an indispensable
daily utility for Hong Kong residents and tourists. An artificial cap will create a
severe supply deficit, resulting in prolonged passenger wait times, excessive surge
pricing, and a downgraded commuting experience—especially during peak hours
and bad weather.
Protecting Flexible Livelihoods: The gig economy thrives on flexibility. Many
ride-hailing operators will be part-time drivers (such as retirees or individuals
seeking supplemental income). Coupled with the government’s proposed
"minimum utilization requirements," a small pool of 10,000 permits will inevitably
be monopolized by full-time fleets. Expanding the quota to 20,000 or more ensures
part-time drivers are not displaced, thereby safeguarding local livelihoods.
Fostering Healthy Industry Competition: Ride-hailing and traditional taxis serve
distinct market segments (on-demand digital booking vs. physical street-hails). A
higher cap of 20,000 provides healthy competition that drives mutual service
improvements across both sectors, rather than using artificial scarcity to protect
outdated monopolies.
Upholding Hong Kong’s Smart City Reputation: As a global financial hub and
tourist destination, Hong Kong must offer seamless, tech-forward transport options
on par with cities like London or Singapore. A severe vehicle shortage risks
frustrating international visitors and undermines the city’s capacity to support

major global mega-events.
Conclusion & Recommendations
An overly conservative cap will disrupt the transport ecosystem and penalize
commuters. The Subcommittee is respectfully requested to increase the permit
limit to at least 20,000 or more for the initial rollout and implement a transparent,
data-driven review mechanism to adjust the quota semi-annually based on realworld fulfillment rates and passenger wait times.
