# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hong Kong general public, journalists, commentators, and engaged citizens following the
ride-hailing regulation debate (LegCo subcommittee sc103). They arrive to find out, quickly:
*who said what, which way they're pulling, and who's really behind each submission.* Most are
reading on a phone, in Traditional Chinese, with limited prior context on the four pieces of
subsidiary legislation. A secondary readership — policy staff and people who may cite the
report — needs the sourcing to hold up under scrutiny.

## Product Purpose

A standalone, published analysis of ~430 public submissions and 7 organisational submissions to
the sc103 subcommittee. It turns a pile of PDFs into a readable verdict: ~90% support regulating
ride-hailing, the real fight is over *how tight* (the 10,000-permit cap above all), and the three
"independent" taxi-trade submissions trace back to one overlapping owner circle. Success is a
reader who lands on the page and, within a minute, understands the stance split, the key fault
lines, and the stakeholder map — and trusts it enough to share or cite it.

## Brand Personality

Investigative and pointed. Data journalism with an analytical edge, not a neutral govt tally.
Three words: **rigorous, plainspoken, unflinching.** It names things directly (the "same owner
circle, three voices" finding; the "查無實體, 疑似一稿兩投" flag) and lets verifiable numbers and
sourcing carry the weight rather than adjectives. Bilingual by nature — primary voice is
Traditional Chinese (with natural Cantonese register where it fits the source), English where the
sources are. Confident but never tabloid; every pointed claim is anchored to a document number or
a cited source.

## Anti-references

- **Partisan / clickbait styling.** No tabloid outrage palette, no loud alarm colours, no
  opinion-blog energy. The edge comes from the findings, not the decoration; loud styling would
  undercut the credibility the report depends on.
- **Generic AI dashboard.** No cards-everywhere reflex, no gradient-hero-plus-stat-grid SaaS
  template, nothing that reads as auto-generated. Stat tiles and cards earn their place or get
  replaced with editorial layout.
- **Government / corporate PDF.** No stiff 官方 bureaucratic block layout, no walls of undifferentiated
  gray text, no hierarchy-free density. This is meant to be *read*, not filed.

## Design Principles

1. **Sourcing in plain sight.** Every claim carries its document number or citation inline. The
   credibility is the product; never make the reader take a finding on faith.
2. **Stance is legible at a glance.** The two-axis tension (regulate-or-not vs. tighter-or-looser)
   and each stakeholder's direction should read from colour, position, and tags before any prose.
3. **Editorial hierarchy over uniform cards.** Lead with the verdict, then the evidence, then the
   stakeholder background. Vary the layout to match the content's weight; resist templating.
4. **Bilingual-first typography.** Traditional Chinese is the primary script; type, line length,
   and rhythm are tuned for CJK reading, with English handled gracefully where sources demand it.
5. **Earn the pointed claims.** The investigative edge is licensed only by the rigour behind it —
   flag what's unverified ("未能核實") as plainly as what's confirmed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1. Watch the stance-bar and
  tag colours (green/red/amber on tinted fills) and the muted gray metadata on dark/warm grounds.
- **Don't encode stance by colour alone** — pair every colour cue with a text label or tag, since
  red/green stance coding fails for colour-blind readers.
- Honour `prefers-reduced-motion` for any motion added later.
- Primary language is `zh-Hant-HK`; keep the `lang` attribute correct and avoid layouts that break
  when Chinese and English mix in the same line.
